🍳 1. Cookware

What kind of pots and pans should you get?

*   **Stainless Steel:** Great for searing meat and sautéing stuff. They last forever but need oil.
*   **Cast Iron:** Awesome for frying, baking, and slow cooking. Keeps heat super well but needs some love (seasoning).
*   **Nonstick (Teflon, Ceramic):** Perfect for eggs and pancakes. Easy to clean, but don't use high heat.
*   **Carbon Steel:** Like a lighter cast iron, good for stir-fries and crepes.
*   **Copper:** Heats up super fast, but expensive. Usually has another metal inside.
*   **Aluminum:** Good for boiling water and general stuff. Often coated with other metals.
*   **Enamel-Coated:** Nice for soups and stews. Looks good and lasts.

**Must-Have Cookware:**

*   Frying pans (small and large)

🧂 8. Utensils: What Material is Best?

*   Silicone: Nonstick pans – Heat-safe, bendy, and you can put them in the dishwasher.
*   Wood: Stirring, serving – Easy on pans but hand-wash only.
*   Stainless Steel: High heat – Lasts forever and you can put them in the dishwasher.
*   Nylon: General cooking – Cheap, but melts if it gets too hot.
*   Bamboo: Like wood – Lightweight and stops germs.

🗂️ 11. Kitchen Layout

Make zones for cooking:

*   Prep Zone: Cutting boards, knives, bowls – near the sink
*   Cooking Zone: Pans, stove – keep spices close
*   Cleaning Zone: Sink, dish rack, trash – organizers under the sink
*   Pantry Zone: Dry food – use labeled bins
*   Storage Zone: Leftovers – near the fridge
*   Baking Zone: Flour, sugar – measuring tools here

Save Space

*   Go up: Use shelves on the wall
*   Pull-out drawers: Easy to get to stuff
*   Lazy Susan: For corners or spices
*   Magnetic strips: For knives or spices
*   Hooks: Hang cups
*   File holder: Stack lids
*   Over-the-door storage: For foil

🧯 13. Stay Safe &amp; Keep It Clean

Clean Your Cookware

*   Season cast iron
*   Don't use metal on nonstick
*   Wash enamel gently
*   Let glass cool

Knives

*   Hone often, sharpen sometimes
*   Wash now (not in the dishwasher)
*   Store in a block or on a strip

Fire

*   Have a fire extinguisher or baking soda
*   Use oven mitts, not towels
*   Clean stove hoods and ovens
*   Don’t leave cooking

🪄 14. Save Time in the Kitchen

*   Prep early: Chop veggies
*   Mise en place: Get stuff out first
*   Label stuff: Find stuff faster
*   Cook big: Freeze half
*   Sharp knives: Faster and safer
*   Clean while you cook: Stops the mess
